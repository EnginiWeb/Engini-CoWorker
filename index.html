The Evolution of Automation & Orchestration

From rigid, rule-based workflows to adaptive, multi-agent orchestration. Each step unlocks more autonomy, context, and business impact.

1980–2023

Deterministic

iPaaS Era

Rule-based flows connect systems with fixed logic and strict schemas. Reliable but brittle when requirements change.

  • • Triggers & pipes; manual exception handling
  • • Heavy upfront mapping & maintenance
  • • Limited adaptability to unstructured inputs

2024

Autonomous

AI Agents

Single agents plan, decide, and use tools. Great for unstructured inputs and adaptive routing—within a single-agent scope.

  • • Perceive → plan → act loops
  • • Tool use on top of legacy systems
  • • Early guardrails & policy enforcement

2025

Sequential

Agentic Workflow

Multiple agents linked in steps to execute complex, multi-stage journeys—each step reasons, then hands off.

  • • Step-level memory & evaluation
  • • Human-in-the-loop approvals
  • • Better resilience with retries & rollbacks

2026 & Beyond

Orchestrated

Agent Orchestration

Coordinated teams of specialized agents share context, negotiate tasks, and collaborate—governed by policies and SLOs.

  • • Multi-agent planning & role assignment
  • • Shared memory, SLAs, & global guardrails
  • • Cross-system, cross-function execution

From Rules → Reasoning

Static mappings give way to contextual reasoning, enabling robust handling of ambiguity.

From Single → Multi-agent

Specialized agents coordinate, parallelize work, and escalate when needed.

From Pipes → Orchestration

Global guardrails, shared memory, and SLOs drive reliable, end-to-end outcomes.
